At Takoyomai, we pride ourselves on crafting each takoyaki and siomai with the utmost care and attention to detail. Our talented cook has combined these traditional recipes with a creative twist, creating an enchanting combination of textures and flavors that will delight your taste buds.

Each takoyaki has a crispy outer shell, soft and salty inside, while our siomai has a delicate outer shell that surrounds a succulent mix of everything. Takoyaki, a popular Japanese street food, typically consists of a ball-shaped batter filled with diced octopus, pickled ginger, and green onion. The batter is cooked in a special takoyaki pan, resulting in a crispy outer shell while maintaining a soft and savory interior. It's often topped with takoyaki sauce, mayonnaise, bonito flakes, and seaweed flakes to enhance the taste. Siomai, on the other hand, is a type of dumpling that originated in China but has become popular in many Asian cuisines, including Filipino cuisine. The delicate outer shell of siomai is made from a thin layer of dough, which is usually filled with a mixture of ground meat (commonly pork), shrimp, and various seasonings. It is then steamed to perfection, resulting in a juicy and flavorful filling encapsulated in a tender wrapper. At Takoyomai, it seems like your skilled cooks have taken these traditional recipes and added their own creative twist, resulting in a unique and enchanting combination of flavors. The crispy outer shell of the takoyaki and the delicate wrapping of the siomai are likely to provide a satisfying texture contrast. Furthermore, the mixture of ingredients in the siomai promises a succulent and mouthwatering experience.
